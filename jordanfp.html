Michael Jeffrey Jordan is the only player, besides Lebron James, to be considered in the GOAT (Greatest Of All Time) debate. He played in the NBA from the mid 80s to early 2000s but is mostly known for his other wordly stretch in the 90s. He is widely known as the kid who came out of college and dominated the NBA. As a rookie he averaged a whopping 28.2 points per game, 5.9 assists, and 6.5 rebounds. He took a bulls team, who had been struggling for a long time, to 6 NBA championship wins with 0 losses in the finals. This insane winning streak included 2 seperate three-peats (3 Championship wins in a row), which is even more insane when you think about all the incredible teams who struggled to win two straight let alone three. In total he averages 30.1 points, 5.3 assists, 6.2 rebounds, 0.8 blocks, and 2.3 steals per game.

37 years ago today, Michael Jordan won the 1984 Rookie of the Year award after completing one of the most dominant rookie seasons in NBA history:
